Tips For A Balcony Garden

Preparing our space, but more improtantly , preparing our mind is the key to a successful and fruitful vegetable season. If you want to see balcony vegetable garden up close head over to this video!

Be Sure To Check The Rules And Regulations For Your Building

The weight of the pots full of soil plus water and last but not least but not least the plant itself.
You can use lightweight containers made from plastic,fiberglass,or fabric grow bags combined with lightweight soil mixes.
If your balcony can endure heavy load,you can use wooden raised beds to use your space more efficient.

Calulate The Time You Have

Maintenance is important, even in a small garden.The is only one simple rule: If you don’t have time, grow low maintenance vegetables.But even they still need more care.
Even if your balcony is big, We still recommend starting small and slow

Choosing The Right Plants For Your Balcony Is The Most Important Thing

There are three most important factors:sunlight , the wind and the heatAlso obviously, choose the right vegetables according to the season. That most vegetables need a minimum of 6 to 8 hours of sun a day.Lots of flowers, and most veggies, if kept well watered, will love it there. Also there are vegetables that prefer more sunlight but can grow in shade - they will grow slower.

Set A Budget Before You Begin

Dont’t Just bye ready-to-plant vegetables unless you want to spent a fortune.Buy seeds or ask friends or gardeners in your community to share seeds. Try to find sale pots or DIY your container. The only thing you need to spend good money on is quality potting soil.Regular ”dirt” is not sterile and bring disease and problems. A balcony vegetable garden with a few pots may not be very expensive , it also totally depends on what you want.

YOU'LL NEED A PLAN IN ADVANCE

Vegetables that fruit from a blossom, such as cucumbers, tomatoes, peppers, and squash don't like shady areas. Plant these in full sun areas that receive the most direct sunlight per day. Root vegetables such as beets, carrots and potatoes can also grow in partially shaded areas with less direct sunlight, but appreciate at least half a day of full sun and some partial shade.

HEALTHY SOIL IS KEY FOR GROWING STRONG

Soil is alive and made up of many parts, including mineral particles, water, organic matter, air, and microorganisms. The balance between these parts is what needs to be sustained for a healthy garden.
Just like all living things, soil needs to be fed in order to provide a nourishing environment to grow your vegetables.

GERMINATING INDOOR & USE MINI GREENHOUSE FOR SOWING EARLY

With cold weather , in order to harvest early in July, it is also necessary to germinate early. If you have a greenhouse, some types of vegetables can be sown at the end of February, mid-March: carrots, pakchoi, some herbs... When the night temperature is above 10 ° C, seed can be sown outdoors. That way you can have way more crop than usual!

POLYCULTURE IS THE HEALTHY WAY OF VEGETABLE GARDENING

Growing several or multiple crops of different plants in the same area gives you greater returns and less work. All plants will boost the other's development splendidly and even protect each other from pests if choose the companion plant carefully.

ROTATE CROPS EACH YEAR

Planting crops in different soil every year prevents the depletion of nutrients and interrupts the cycles of pests and diseases so the garden soil stays healthy.
